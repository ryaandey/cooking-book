RASMALAI

Rasmalai is a delicious Indian dessert made of soft cheese dumplings (chhena) soaked in sweet, flavored milk, often garnished with pistachios and saffron, making it a rich and indulgent treat.

Ingredients:

For the cheese balls:

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder

For the milk syrup:

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)
  • A few drops of rose water (optional)
  • Chopped pistachios and almonds for garnish


Instructions:

  • Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan. Once it comes to a boil, reduce the heat and add lemon juice or vinegar, stirring gently until the milk curdles. Remove from heat and strain the curds through a muslin or cheese cloth to separate the whey. Rinse the chhena under cold water to remove the sourness and let it drain for about 30 minutes.

  • Transfer the chhena to a plate and knead it well for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and forms a soft dough. You can add all-purpose flour and baking powder for binding if desired.

  • Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a marble) and flatten them slightly to form discs.

  • In a heavy pan, boil the milk and let it reduce to about half its volume. Stir occasionally to prevent it from sticking. Add sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands, and mix well. Cook for an additional 5-10 minutes until it thickens slightly.

  • In a separate pot, boil water and gently add the cheese balls. Cook them for about 10-12 minutes until they double in size. Remove them from the water and let them cool slightly.

  • Place the cooked cheese balls in the warm milk syrup and let them soak for at least 1-2 hours in the refrigerator.

  • Garnish with chopped pistachios and almonds before serving. Rasmalai can be served chilled or at room temperature.
