GULAB JAMUN

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from deep-fried dough balls soaked in sweet syrup, offering a delightful combination of textures and flavors that melt in your mouth.



Ingredients:

  • 1 cup khoya (dried milk)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon milk (or as needed)
  • Ghee or oil for frying

For the syrup:

  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few drops of rose water (optional)


Instructions:

  • In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil, then add cardamom powder and rose water. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Set aside.

  • In a bowl, mix khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking powder. Add milk little by little to form a smooth and soft dough. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes.

  • Grease your palms and divide the dough into small portions. Roll them into smooth balls (about the size of a marble).

  • Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan. Fry the balls on low to medium heat, turning them gently until they turn golden brown. Ensure they cook evenly.

  • Once fried, remove the balls and drain excess oil. Immediately place them in the warm sugar syrup. Allow them to soak for at least 30 minutes.

  • Gulab Jamun can be served warm or at room temperature, garnished with nuts if desired.
