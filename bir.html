CHICKEN BIRYANI

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish layered with marinated meat or vegetables, often enriched with spices and herbs, making it a beloved culinary masterpiece across the Indian subcontinent.



Ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Whole spices (2-3 cloves, 2-3 cardamom pods, 1-inch cinnamon stick, and bay leaf)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil or ghee


Instructions:

  • In a bowl, mix the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, salt, and half the chopped mint and coriander. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.

  • Rinse the basmati rice until the water runs clear. Boil water in a pot, add whole spices and salt, then add the rice. Cook until it's about 70% done, then drain and set aside.

  • In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing later.

  • Add the marinated chicken to the pot, followed by the tomatoes. Cook until the chicken is tender and the oil separates.

  • Spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken, sprinkle garam masala, and layer the remaining rice on top. Garnish with fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves. Drizzle some more ghee on top.

  • Cover the pot tightly with a lid. Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes (you can use a heavy pan underneath to avoid direct heat).

  • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving. Enjoy with raita or salad!
